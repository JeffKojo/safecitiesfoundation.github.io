What We Do

Safer streets, built with communities

From focus areas that guide our advocacy to the signs, crossings, and traffic-calming measures already on the ground — this is the work transforming how Kumasi moves.

Focus Areas

Six interconnected programs

Driving sustainable, people-centred urban change across Ghana and the wider African continent.

Urban Mobility Advocacy

We campaign for safe, affordable, and sustainable transportation systems — from Bus Rapid Transit corridors and pedestrian walkways to cycling infrastructure — that serve all Ghanaians equally and prioritise non-motorised transport.

Community-Led Spatial Planning

We embed directly into communities to co-design public spaces, markets, transit hubs, and streetscapes — ensuring local voices and indigenous knowledge shape the urban environments they inhabit and depend on.

Data-Driven Research

We collect, analyse, and publish urban mobility data to inform evidence-based policy. Our work on commuter patterns, pedestrian safety hotspots, and spatial inequity drives decision-making at the city and national level.

Policy Engagement

We engage government ministries, city authorities, and development partners to champion transformative policies — from inclusive zoning codes to road safety legislation and integrated transport master plans for Ghanaian cities.

Capacity Building

We run training programmes, fellowships, and workshops to develop the next generation of African urban practitioners — planners, advocates, data scientists, and community leaders who will shape our cities from within.

Knowledge Exchange

We facilitate South-South learning between African cities, connecting urban practitioners and policymakers across the continent to share lessons, best practices, and innovations in mobility and spatial planning.
