Who We Are

Transforming how African cities move and grow

Safe Cities Foundation is a Ghanaian non-governmental organisation working at the intersection of urban mobility, spatial planning, and civic advocacy. We believe the future of African cities must be shaped by the people who live in them — not by car-centric legacies inherited from elsewhere.

From the streets of Kumasi to communities across sub-Saharan Africa, we partner with governments, development institutions, community groups, and residents to reshape the urban experience through research, advocacy, and co-design.

More About Us Work With Us →

Our Vision

A future where African cities are designed for people — compact, resilient, and inclusive urban spaces where every citizen can thrive.

Our Mission

To transform African urban mobility and spatial planning through data-driven advocacy, community-led design, and the optimisation of shared urban spaces for all.

What We Do

Our Focus Areas

Interconnected programs driving sustainable, people-centred urban change across Ghana and the wider African continent.

Urban Mobility Advocacy

We campaign for safe, affordable, and sustainable transportation systems — from Bus Rapid Transit corridors and pedestrian walkways to cycling infrastructure — that serve all Ghanaians equally.

Learn more

Community-Led Spatial Planning

We embed directly into communities to co-design public spaces, markets, transit hubs, and streetscapes — ensuring local voices and indigenous knowledge shape the urban environments they inhabit.

Learn more

Data-Driven Research

We collect, analyse, and publish urban mobility data to inform evidence-based policy — from commuter patterns to pedestrian safety hotspots and spatial inequity.
